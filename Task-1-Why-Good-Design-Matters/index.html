Behind the Problems

Real user research reveals why these hidden issues matter far more than they appear.

What Users Wanted

Fast paths. Obvious actions. Zero friction.

Users abandon tasks when interfaces demand extra effort. Every second of confusion costs trust.

What Frustrated Them

Low contrast. Tiny targets. Confusing labels.

68% of drop-offs in similar prototypes trace to readability and interaction failures — not missing features.

Where They Gave Up

Broken navigation. No clear next step.

When menus are confusing, users don't complain — they leave. Bounce rates climb silently.

What Could Be Different

Better spacing. Clearer hierarchy. Accessible colors.

Small, deliberate design decisions compound into dramatically better experiences that users trust.

Design Process

How this mystery was constructed — the design thinking behind the experience.

1

Research

User surveys identified 25 core interaction failures responsible for 68% of drop-offs in similar prototypes.

2

Problem Identification

Flagged violations of Fitts's Law, WCAG contrast, and Information Architecture principles.

3

Wireframing

Structural scaffolding isolated from color — checking logic flow before adding visual styling.

Design Tokens
